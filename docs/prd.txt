The 130-hour productivity savings represents the cumulative time an individual employee regains annually by utilizing Prezent Energy's on-demand mobile charging instead of traditional fixed infrastructure.
This efficiency gain is driven by three primary factors identified in the sources:
Elimination of the "Charging Shuffle": Traditional corporate campuses often have a limited number of fixed charging stations relative to the number of EV-driving employees. This forces workers to interrupt their day to search for an open spot or move their vehicle mid-day once it has finished charging to avoid idle fees or accommodate colleagues.
On-Demand Autonomous Delivery: Prezent Energy’s VoltBot autonomous robots navigate directly to a vehicle's GPS coordinates wherever it is parked in the lot. Because the "power comes to the car" rather than the "car searching for power," employees do not spend time walking to distant charging bays or managing vehicle rotations.
Frictionless Scheduling: Employees use a simple mobile app to book a morning or evening slot, allowing them to remain focused on their core business priorities while the charging happens "hands-off" in the background.
For large employers, particularly FAANG campuses and enterprise sites, this model allows them to "ingest" these lost hours back into productive tasks while providing 100% employee coverage. This serves as a significant competitive advantage in talent markets by eliminating commute range anxiety and the daily stress of competing for limited charging spots
